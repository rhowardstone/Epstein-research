## LEAD 7: EPSTEIN'S "COMPUTERIZED DATABASE" TO TRACK VICTIMS

### Prior Claim
Epstein maintained "a computerized database to track the availability and movements of women and girls."

### Source Documents Found
- **[EFTA00016836](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00016836.pdf)** - Dataset 8, `/datasets/dataset8/VOL00008/IMAGES/0002/EFTA00016836.pdf`
- **[EFTA00014380](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00014380.pdf)** - Dataset 8, `/datasets/dataset8/VOL00008/IMAGES/0002/EFTA00014380.pdf`
- **[EFTA00010202](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00010202.pdf)** - Referenced in database queries regarding "Epstein SW Database"

### What the Documents Actually Say

**[EFTA00016836](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00016836.pdf)** is a New York Times article titled "Lawsuit Claims Epstein Trafficked Girls in Caribbean Until 2018," shared internally by SDNY prosecutors. It is NOT an internal DOJ investigative finding. The database claim originates from the U.S. Virgin Islands Attorney General Denise George's civil lawsuit. The article states that Epstein "kept a computerized database to track the availability and movements of women and girls" -- but this is a CLAIM FROM A CIVIL LAWSUIT, not a finding from the DOJ's criminal investigation.

**[EFTA00014380](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00014380.pdf)** references "US v. Epstein (SW database)" in email headers. This refers to the **Relativity e-discovery platform** used by USAO-SDNY to manage seized evidence. It involves production stamping and data loading for USAO-SDFL files. This is a litigation management tool, not a victim-tracking database.

**[EFTA00010202](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00010202.pdf)** similarly references "Epstein SW Database" in the context of Relativity access for attorneys.

### VERIFIED
- The USVI AG's civil lawsuit (filed by Denise George) alleged Epstein maintained a computerized database to track victims
- This allegation was reported by the NYT and circulated within SDNY
- The "Epstein SW database" referenced in DOJ emails is the Relativity e-discovery platform

### UNVERIFIED
- No DOJ document in the 218GB file set independently confirms the existence of a victim-tracking database
- No seized device or forensic report references discovering such a database
- The USVI AG lawsuit allegation has not been corroborated by DOJ criminal investigators in any document found

### CORRECTION FROM PRIOR INVESTIGATION
The prior investigation conflated two separate things: (1) a claim from a civil lawsuit about a victim-tracking database, and (2) the government's own Relativity e-discovery database used to manage case evidence. These are entirely different systems. The prior characterization that DOJ investigators discovered a victim-tracking database is **NOT supported** by the source documents.

### MISSING
- No forensic report from CART (Computer Analysis Response Team) mentions discovering a database matching this description on any of the 60+ seized devices
- If the USVI AG had independent evidence of such a database, that evidence is not present in these federal files

## LEAD 8: FLIGHT LOG MODIFICATIONS BY PILOT LARRY RODGERS

### Prior Claim
Epstein's pilot Larry Rodgers modified flight logs to remove or alter passenger names, suggesting deliberate concealment.

### Source Documents Found
- **[EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf)** - Dataset 12, `/datasets/dataset12/VOL00012/IMAGES/0001/EFTA02731168.pdf` (32-page SDNY Prosecution Memorandum, dated April 10, 2020, for charging Ghislaine Maxwell)

### What the Document Actually Says

[EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf) is marked "Privileged and Confidential -- Attorney Work Product / Do Not Disseminate Outside of the SDNY USAO."

Page 8 states, verbatim:

> "did not personally fly, and it does not include the names of passengers whose names he did not know at the time of a flight. When Rodgers learned the name of a passenger who had previously flown on other flights, Rodgers would use that passenger's name going forward but would not go back to add in that passenger's name on prior flight logs."

Page 7 establishes that Rodgers was employed by Epstein from 1991 to 2019 and kept logs of every flight with date, airports, and passenger names when known.

Page 5 identifies three specific flights where a victim was a minor passenger (ages 16-17).

### VERIFIED
- Larry Rodgers kept flight logs spanning 1991-2019
- Rodgers did NOT retroactively update flight logs when he later learned a passenger's name
- When Rodgers learned a name, he used it going forward on future flights
- Passenger names were sometimes unknown at the time of a flight and recorded generically or omitted
- Three specific flights with a minor victim are documented

### UNVERIFIED
- There is NO evidence in [EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf) that Rodgers deliberately removed, altered, or tampered with existing flight log entries
- There is no evidence of anyone instructing Rodgers to modify logs

### CORRECTION FROM PRIOR INVESTIGATION
The prior characterization overstated the significance. Rodgers did NOT "modify" flight logs. He simply did not go back to retroactively add names he learned later -- a routine logging practice, not evidence of tampering or concealment. The prosecution memo presents this as a limitation of the logs' completeness, not as evidence of obstruction. The prior investigation's framing of this as sinister flight log modification is **NOT supported** by the source document.

### NEW FINDINGS FROM [EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf)
- Page 26: "we have not received most of the materials seized in July and August of 2019, which comprise many terabytes of data"
- Page 26: "we require significant assistance from the FBI that to date has not been forthcoming"
- The prosecution memo was drafted to charge Maxwell even though prosecutors lacked access to most seized digital evidence
- Page 10: Maxwell said to a victim's sibling: "I'll leave you guys to it" before leaving victim alone with Epstein
- Page 2: Epstein and Maxwell recruited a victim by approaching her walking her dog and posing as scholarship donors

## LEAD 9: 12 TERABYTES OF RAW DEVICE DATA (FBI-USAO STANDOFF)

### Prior Claim
60+ devices were seized containing terabytes of data, and there was a massive breakdown between FBI CART and USAO-SDNY in processing this evidence.

### Source Documents Found
- **[EFTA00009941](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00009941.pdf)** - Dataset 8, `/datasets/dataset8/VOL00008/IMAGES/0001/EFTA00009941.pdf` (11-page email chain, Feb-Jul 2020, between USAO-SDNY and FBI NY CART)
- **[EFTA00009802](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00009802.pdf)** - Dataset 8 (forwarded email with explicit frustration from prosecutors)
- **[EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf)** - Dataset 12 (prosecution memo confirming evidence gap, page 26)

### What the Documents Actually Say

**[EFTA00009941](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00009941.pdf)** is an extensive email chain documenting escalating frustration between SDNY prosecutors and the FBI's Computer Analysis Response Team (CART).

Key verbatim quotes:

FBI CART Coordinator (page 6, id 24222):
> "Just to be clear. The US Attorney's Office (or it's contractors) are not 'processing' anything. You are taking files that I will be extracting from processed evidence and putting them into an E-Discovery tool (Relativity) to do a taint review."
> "Relativity is NOT a forensic tool. It is incapable of dealing with many things that are found forensically on a computer like free space, slack space, and system files."

FBI CART on device scope (page 4, id 19682):
> References "terabytes worth of data over multiple forms of digital evidence. Phones, tablets, loose media, cameras, DVRs, servers, laptops, and desktop computers. We have gotten past encryption on multiple devices."

FBI CART on COVID delays (page 10, id 35782):
> "Sorry for the delay, they reduced us to 1 day a week, so things have been stretched out by a factor of 5."

FBI CART on data volume (page 5, id 23708):
> "They are tearing out our old network and giving us a new one, they mandated we delete old stuff (about 400 TB worth)."

USAO prosecutors could not determine which devices were represented in the data received:
> "We've received and reviewed the hard drive you provided last week. Unfortunately it's still not possible for us to determine which devices have been fully 100% included in that drive."

On the data format problems:
> "The data we've received has no way to put any emails together with their attachments" and the control numbers did not match, making cross-referencing impossible.

On Manhattan evidence: 9 IDE hard drives from Manhattan apartment = 3 copies of 3 drives from the July 2007 search.

Device count: approximately 40 devices from the NY mansion, 25+ from the Virgin Islands including servers.

**[EFTA00009802](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00009802.pdf)** contains the now-famous internal forwarded email (page 5, id 32678):
> "Notwithstanding their many promises to us about quick and effective processing of the 60+ devices they seized, the FBI is completely fucking us on this."

**[EFTA02731168](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731168.pdf)** page 26 confirms from the prosecution memo itself:
> "we have not received most of the materials seized in July and August of 2019, which comprise many terabytes of data"

### VERIFIED
- 60+ devices were seized from NY mansion and USVI properties
- The data comprised "many terabytes" across phones, tablets, loose media, cameras, DVRs, servers, laptops, and desktops
- FBI CART broke encryption on multiple devices
- Relativity was confirmed inadequate for forensic evidence review
- COVID reduced CART operations to 1 day per week, stretching timelines by factor of 5
- As of April 2020, USAO-SDNY still did not have a basic device inventory
- Over 1 million documents received were largely unusable due to broken metadata
- USAO prosecutors explicitly described the FBI as "completely fucking us" on evidence processing
- The Maxwell prosecution memo was drafted without access to most seized digital evidence
- The 2007 Manhattan hard drives were included (3 copies of 3 drives)

### UNVERIFIED
- The total exact data volume (the "12 TB" figure cited in prior reports) is not specified precisely in these documents; they say "many terabytes" and "terabytes worth of data"
- Whether the evidence processing breakdown was intentional obstruction versus bureaucratic/resource failure cannot be determined from these documents alone

### NEW FINDINGS
- The FBI's own network was simultaneously undergoing replacement, with 400 TB of old data being deleted
- The CART coordinator was a "Senior Forensic Examiner" who pushed back on how the data was being handled, indicating genuine technical disagreements rather than mere negligence
- The spreadsheet tracking system broke down repeatedly -- prosecutors could not match folders to devices
- DVRs (digital video recorders) are specifically mentioned among seized devices, indicating surveillance footage was captured
- The email chain spans February 2020 through July 2020, showing six months of failed coordination

### MISSING
- No final resolution document showing whether all device data was eventually processed
- No inventory or forensic report cataloguing what was ultimately recovered from the terabytes of data
- No record of what was found on the DVRs or surveillance footage
