The 1953 Trust contains a provision that names HBRK Associates, Inc. by name — the **only** corporate entity singled out in the clause:

> "No bequest to any Beneficiary... who, during my lifetime, was employed by or provided services to me or any entity that was owned directly or indirectly by me during my lifetime or **HBRK Associates, Inc.**... shall be distributed to said Beneficiary prior to the expiration of **two years following the date of my death**."

Any beneficiary who voluntarily quit or was terminated for misconduct within two years would **forfeit their entire bequest**. The trust further required continued employment of all HBRK/Indyke employees for two years post-death, with compensation from trust assets.

**Effect:** Every HBRK employee, every island staff member, every household manager — anyone who worked for HBRK — had financial incentive to remain employed and silent for exactly two years after August 10, 2019.

## 14. COUNTER-STRATEGIES

### If He Invokes the Fifth

- The Fifth Amendment does NOT protect pre-existing documents — only testimony
- Subpoena HBRK corporate records directly; HBRK Associates Inc. (a corporation) has no Fifth Amendment privilege
- Subpoena TD Bank, Deutsche Bank, JPMorgan records independently of Kahn

### If He Claims Attorney-Client Privilege

- Kahn is **not a lawyer** — he is an accountant. Attorney-client privilege does not apply to his communications.
- The accountant-client privilege does NOT exist under federal law. Only a few states recognize it, and it does not apply in federal proceedings.
- His co-executor Indyke may attempt to assert privilege over joint communications, but the crime-fraud exception applies to communications furthering ongoing criminal activity.

### If He Claims Limited Knowledge

- His own emails show he tracked every wire, managed every entity, chased every payment
- He personally withdrew $50,000 in cash (triggering a CTR)
- He managed payroll for all island employees
- He negotiated construction contracts down to the dollar
- FBI documents call him the primary wire authorization contact

### If He Blames Indyke

- Their roles were complementary, not hierarchical: Indyke handled legal structure, Kahn handled financial operations
- Both were co-trustees, co-executors, and co-beneficiaries
- The lock-in provision in the 1953 Trust treated them as an integrated unit
