### What the documents do NOT show

They do not show that Lutnick paid Epstein. They do not show that Epstein retained any interest in 11 East 71st after 1996. Real-estate documents are mediated through trusts and not all participants in a nominal transfer have substantive economic interest. They also do not show the actual $7.6M figure or the $4M mortgage in the EFTA corpus — those are derived from external real-estate journalism. What the documents **do** show is that the entity "11 East 71st Street Trust" continued to receive correspondence in Epstein's name as late as 2005.

### Lutnick's testimony

> Q: Did you know at the time that you purchased the home that the home had previously been owned by Jeffrey Epstein and Les Wexner?
> A: I don't know. I knew I bought it from Charles de Gunzburg. ... When I bought the house, I didn't know. I don't recall knowing. (Transcript p.40)

> Q: That Jeffrey Epstein previously owned your home?
> A: Yes. ... I don't know that to be true. (p.42)

Public real-estate records and the corpus contradict the "I don't know" framing. Wexner's SAM Conversion Corp. owned 11 E 71st with Epstein as VP, and a trust bearing the address remained linked to Epstein through 2005.

## 3. THE THREE ACKNOWLEDGED INTERACTIONS — AND WHAT THE EMAILS SHOW

Lutnick's opening statement (transcript pp.9–11) divides his Epstein contact into "three occasions in which I interacted with Epstein in person. Each and every one was meaningless and inconsequential."

### Interaction #1 — 2005 coffee + house tour

Lutnick describes a 10–15 minute coffee in Epstein's living room shortly after moving in, followed by a tour that ended when Epstein opened a door to reveal a massage table with candles around it. Lutnick asked how often he had massages; Epstein responded "Every day, and the right kind of massage." Lutnick and his wife left, and on the walk back next door agreed he would "avoid him" (transcript pp.12–15, 44–47).

### Interaction #2 — May 1, 2011 ("scaffolding")

Lutnick testified he stood "in the foyer with my dog" while Epstein "told me what he had to say. ... My best recollection was it had to do with scaffolding" (transcript p.32–33). This was confirmed by both calendar entries and email traffic in the corpus:

### What the May 2011 thread shows that Lutnick's testimony does NOT

In the call rotation [EFTA00435530](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00435530), Lutnick was added to the same outbound call list as Leon Black, Boris Nikolic, Jes Staley, and John Brockman — the same names that recur as Epstein's repeat callers in 2011. Lutnick's testimony characterizes the call as inconvenience-driven ("we were unable to connect by phone" — p.32). The corpus shows it as a routine entry on Epstein's weekly call sheet, on the same list as named co-conspirators (Staley) and known associates (Black).

### Lutnick's testimony on how the invitation came about

> "What I still cannot understand is this November 20, how or why, without any communication for years would he inexplicably know where I'm going. It's unsettling, actually." (transcript p.22)

The corpus contradicts this framing in two specific ways:
1. **Lutnick personally emailed Epstein on Dec 19, 2012** with travel plans, captain logistics, the names of his guests (Michael and Marcy Lehrman), and the ages of all eight children involved ([EFTA00399319](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00399319)). They do not show that anything inappropriate happened during the lunch itself. Lutnick testified no women under 25 were present and he saw nothing unusual (transcript pp.24, 84). The DOJ has released photographs of Lutnick on Little Saint James from the December 2012 visit ([NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/congress/howard-lutnick-jeffrey-epstein-island-rcna258333)), confirming the visit but not depicting misconduct. There is no documentary evidence that Lutnick visited the island on any other occasion.

### Lutnick's testimony on AdFin

> Q: When this agreement was signed in December of 2012, did you know that Mr. Epstein was also a signatory to the agreement?
> A: No. (Transcript p.87)

> "I don't think I heard about this investment till 2013, May. I think the pitch was made to me in May of 2013, from the document. So it wasn't in December." (p.88)

Lutnick's counsel proffered to the committee that "when Cantor Fitzgerald Venture Capital signed these documents, it was in ... 2013 in the spring" — not the document's December 28, 2012 date. The committee did not have time to verify this proffer in the room and accepted it on the record (transcript p.87).

### What the AdFin documents DO and do NOT show

They show a contemporaneous business co-investment in which Epstein and Lutnick signed consecutive counterpart signature pages to the same series-A round. They do not show Lutnick was a personal investor (he signed on behalf of "CVAFH I LLC," a Cantor Ventures SPV). They do not show the two men negotiated directly at signing — they show only that they ended up with adjacent pages in the same closing binder.

The May 2018 email exchange (six and a half years after the lunch, Lutnick's only directly-acknowledged email exchange with Epstein in the transcript) shows Lutnick *did* respond personally to Epstein (signed "HWL") on AdFin's financial prospects — a substantive business communication six months before the Miami Herald exposé and 14 months before Epstein's federal arrest.

### What is significant

In the transcript Lutnick characterized his entire 2018 contact with Epstein as a single four-message exchange "in one day once" (transcript p.18). The Frick-expansion / AdFin / Pierre Hotel exchange ([EFTA01050718](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01050718) → [EFTA01050772](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01050772)) is that exchange. He responded personally rather than via his assistant Matthew Gilbert. Six months later Epstein was on the cover of the Miami Herald.

## 12. COUNTER-STRATEGIES — HOW LUTNICK'S TEAM IS LIKELY TO FRAME GAPS

### The "I, Howard Lutnick" reinterpretation

The transcript's central rhetorical move was Lutnick's reinterpretation of "I was never in the room with him socially" to mean "I, Howard Lutnick, unaccompanied by my wife, was never alone in a room with him" (transcript pp.48–53). The minority counsel pressed this for ten transcript pages without obtaining a literal-grammatical concession.

**Counter:** This is a reading of "I" that would not survive any standard of English usage. The Pod Force One transcript already contains a parallel construction ("I was never in the room with him for business") — businesses are not conducted alone. The natural reading is "I, Howard Lutnick" as the speaker — meaning the simple subject of the verb — not "I as opposed to we." Future questioning should not concede this reinterpretation but instead press the literal grammatical reading and ask Lutnick to either (a) acknowledge the original podcast statement was inaccurate or (b) explain why his October 2025 listeners should have inferred the qualifier.

### "I was just speculating for a podcast"

On the "greatest blackmailer ever" claim and the "videos in exchange for the plea deal" claim, Lutnick repeatedly invoked "I was just speculating for a podcast" (transcript pp.18, 54–55, 73–74).

**Counter:** This frame is unstable. Either Lutnick was speculating wildly about Epstein's behavior with no factual basis — in which case the speculative claims he chose to broadcast were specifically about blackmail, videos, and the DOJ plea deal — or he was drawing on actual knowledge. If the former, the relevant follow-up is: "Why did you choose to speculate, on a public podcast, in those specific lurid directions rather than any other?" If the latter: identify the knowledge source.

### "Inexplicable and unsettling"

Lutnick used "inexplicable and unsettling" as a stock phrase at least six times to characterize Epstein's apparent knowledge of his travel plans and the AdFin signature-page co-presence.

**Counter:** The corpus provides a direct explanation that is not inexplicable: Lutnick personally emailed Epstein with his travel plans on Dec 19, 2012 ([EFTA00399319](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00399319)). The "inexplicable" framing presupposes Lutnick did not initiate; the contemporaneous email shows he did.

### Executive privilege without invocation

Lutnick's counsel deferred Trump-related questions to "executive privilege" but did not invoke it through the Rule 16(c)(1) process — and ultimately reframed as "asked and answered."

**Counter:** The committee can demand a written privilege log identifying each topic withheld and the formal basis. Failure to provide that log means the questions are simply being refused, not privileged — exposing the witness to potential contempt if a future deposition reaches them under compulsion.

### "Matthew Gilbert handled everything"

Lutnick repeatedly stated that emails addressed to "Lutnick, Howard" went to his longtime assistant Matthew Gilbert — "they were not — meaning, I would never see them" (transcript p.22).

**Counter:** Three of the May 2018 emails are signed "HWL" — Lutnick himself. Lutnick conceded these were him (transcript p.29). The "Matthew Gilbert handled all of it" framing therefore does not cover the substantive 2018 communications. Future questioning should focus exclusively on the HWL-signed exchanges.

### Conflict Notes

- **The "$10 sale" allegation.** The Andriesz tip (Tier 3 as an allegation) is corroborated as a deed-record fact (Tier 1) — the $10 nominal consideration is recorded on the actual deed, per [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/howard-lutnick-epstein-ties-2099660) (Tier 2). What is NOT corroborated is the implied criminality. A $10 deed transfer following a trust restructuring is standard real-estate practice. The actual purchase price was $7.6M (transfer-tax records). The fact pattern is real; the inference of money laundering remains unsubstantiated.

- **Lutnick's "I" interpretation.** Two readings of his Pod Force One statement coexist in the record: (a) the natural-language reading on which most listeners and journalists relied (he was claiming he had no in-person contact with Epstein after 2005); (b) Lutnick's May 2026 reinterpretation (he was claiming only that he, alone, without his wife, never set foot in a room with Epstein). Reading (a) is contradicted by the corpus. Reading (b) is consistent with the literal Latin "I" — but inconsistent with the verbal context "for business, or even philanthropy" which describes activities Lutnick conducted *with* his wife at Dubin Breast Center events.

- **"Glenn Dubin may stop by."** The May 1, 2011 day-of-schedule ([EFTA00307005](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00307005)) places Lutnick's 5pm appointment 90 minutes before a Woody Allen dinner at which "Glenn Dubin may stop by." Lutnick claims he was in the foyer for the scaffolding discussion only (transcript p.32–33). Whether Dubin or Lutnick overlapped is not documentary in the corpus — but the same-day rotation matters for the broader picture of Lutnick's placement in Epstein's social network.

## 14. NEWSWORTHY FINDINGS THE TRANSCRIPT SURFACES

Five findings emerge most clearly from cross-referencing the transcript with the corpus:

1. **Lutnick personally initiated the December 2012 island contact.** The committee's framing during questioning treated the 2012 visit as an Epstein-initiated approach. The corpus ([EFTA00399319](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00399319), [EFTA02151286](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA02151286)) shows Lutnick personally emailed Epstein "Hi Jeff" with travel plans, captain logistics, guests by name, and children's ages on Dec 19, 2012. He originally proposed *dinner*, not lunch. This single email transforms the visit from "inexplicable invitation" into active coordination.

2. **The deed chain at 11 East 71st Street is a fact pattern, not a conspiracy theory.** The $10 sale-price story from the Andriesz NTOC tip — Wexner's SAM Conversion Corp. → 11 East 71st Street Trust (Epstein trustee) → Comet Trust → Lutnick — is documented in public real-estate records and confirmed in corpus material ([EFTA00263278](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00263278), [EFTA00263107](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00263107)). A trust by that exact address persisted in Epstein's correspondence through 2005 ([EFTA01304561 p.13](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01304561) and seven duplicates).

3. **The FBI has substantially more on Lutnick than the single NTOC tip on the Prominent Names page.** The April 23, 2021 Guardian complaint ([EFTA01249210](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01249210)) records 26 NY case-file hits for Lutnick, including a restricted closed money-laundering case (272D-NY-306108) and a RICO real-estate control file (194-NY-C277070-B). DIVS returned 34 results including three SARs. The FBI declined to open an investigation in January 2025 despite all of this. Congress has never seen those underlying case files; the May 6 interview did not press on them.

4. **The May 13, 2019 iMessage about Trump.** Two months before Epstein's arrest, an iMessage from Epstein's phone ([HOUSE_OVERSIGHT_027694](https://epstein-data.com/HOUSE_OVERSIGHT_027694)) reads: "Trump is coming to Lutnick's house Thursday." Lutnick refused to discuss Trump conversations without formally invoking executive privilege. The iMessage itself was not introduced as an exhibit during the interview.

5. **The AdFin co-signature, the May 2018 emails, and the Christmas Eve TopTenDB attachment together break the "meaningless and inconsequential" framing.** Lutnick signed the AdFin Series A Preferred Stock Purchase Agreement on a counterpart page directly adjacent to Epstein's signature for Southern Trust Company ([EFTA00289560 pp.20–21](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00289560)). The morning after the December 2012 island lunch, Epstein sent Lutnick a Deutsche Bank document called "TopTenDB.pdf" ([EFTA00398744](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00398744)). In May 2018, six months before the Miami Herald series, Lutnick personally — not through Gilbert — emailed Epstein with substantive business analysis ([EFTA01050772](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01050772)). None of these are "inconsequential."

## 15. WHAT THE TRANSCRIPT IS GOOD FOR

Despite its limitations as a voluntary off-camera transcribed interview, the May 6 record establishes several useful facts for the public record:

- **Lutnick conceded the 2012 island visit happened.** Pre-transcript, this was contested; post-transcript, it is on the record (pp.21, 24, 50).
- **He conceded the 2011 scaffolding meeting happened.** Same (pp.32–33).
- **He conceded the May 2018 email exchange was him personally.** Same (p.29).
- **He conceded he attended the December 2012 lunch as a "social lunch" with his family** (p.50) — directly contradicting his October 2025 podcast claim that he was "never in the room with him socially."
- **He conceded he was speculating on Pod Force One** about Epstein being a blackmailer, the existence of videos, and the trade-for-plea-deal theory (pp.54–55, 73–74). That concession is the substantive abandonment of his most newsworthy public statement about Epstein.

The record is therefore valuable as a *baseline of admitted contact* — the floor below which Lutnick cannot retreat in any subsequent proceeding.
