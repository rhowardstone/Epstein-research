## TL;DR

The DOJ EFTA corpus documents a fifteen-year relationship between Jeffrey Epstein and **Terje Rød-Larsen**, the Norwegian diplomat, Oslo Accords architect, and former president of the International Peace Institute. The records show repeated overlap between Epstein, IPI, UN-adjacent diplomatic networks, Gates Foundation–linked funding discussions, and high-level social access. They also show the relationship extending into Rød-Larsen family matters: travel logistics; the use of Epstein's 22 Avenue Foch apartment by Terje's adult daughter Hilde; gifts to Edward and Emma Juul Rød-Larsen; Edward's Christie's internship and NYU application support; and Epstein's August 2019 will. The final will, executed two days before Epstein's death, **increased Emma's earlier-draft bequest from $2 million to $5 million and added Edward at $5 million** — a doubled and expanded allocation in the final document.

The corpus does not establish that Edward or Emma knew about Epstein's trafficking crimes, received estate money, or committed wrongdoing. It also does not contain any email *from* Mona Juul to Epstein, though it documents repeated family, travel, and diplomatic overlap involving her name and position.

On **9 February 2026**, Norway's economic-crime authority Økokrim announced that Mona Juul had been charged with **aggravated corruption** and Terje Rød-Larsen with **contribution to aggravated corruption**; both deny criminal liability, and the investigation remains ongoing. On **30 April 2026**, Norwegian media reported that Edward Juul Rød-Larsen had died at age 25.

Records concerning Edward and Emma are included in this report because they illuminate Epstein's access to and influence around a diplomatic family; they are not presented as evidence of wrongdoing by either child.

## 2. Visa & Immigration Interventions

The clearest consular-process episode in the Rød-Larsen material is the **November 2012 Norwegian visa intervention**, in which Epstein used UN-affiliated diplomatic channels to attempt to short-circuit a consular process. This is documented in detail in the [French Connection investigation](../FRENCH_CONNECTION_INVESTIGATION.md#42-fabrice-aidans-role) and is summarised here only for cross-reference:

- **27 November 2012, 12:39 PM** — Epstein forwards a consular email about a Norway visa application to Fabrice Aidan (UN Office of Legal Affairs / IPI) and **Terje Rød-Larsen**, asking *"not sure why?"* (Epstein's "not sure why?" body preserved within the email chain at [EFTA01763914](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01763914); the forward itself is at [EFTA01976003](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01976003))
- **27 November 2012, 6:29 PM** — Rød-Larsen replies: *"Because this is what normally is required! And then it takes at least two weeks. She should have gone there a week ago when everything was ok. Have to to everything once again. Stay put."* ([EFTA01763914](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01763914))
- A parallel thread exists in [EFTA01975606](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01975606) where Epstein asks Aidan: *"is waiting at the consulate for her visa. can you help"*
- **Later that day** — Epstein replies in the chain: *"no worry, it was closed for thanksgiving"* ([EFTA01975031](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA01975031)), suggesting Epstein himself dismissed the urgency after Rød-Larsen's pushback — strengthening the *"redirected, not facilitated"* characterization.

**What the document does NOT show:** the visa applicant's name, her purpose of travel, or whether she actually obtained a Norwegian visa. Rød-Larsen's reply *redirects* rather than facilitates — he tells Epstein the consular requirement is normal and the application was filed too late. The corpus does not show Rød-Larsen taking any action to override consular procedure.

**Reliability tier:** Tier 1 (contemporaneous email correspondence within DOJ production).

## 7. Pattern of Behavior — what the corpus shows

Reading the corpus EFTAs together, the documentary record shows three concentric layers of relationship:

1. **Operational diplomacy.** The records show IPI's institutional position — physical adjacency to UN headquarters, UN-Secretary-General-as-honorary-chair branding, Gates Foundation funding, and a recurring stream of UN/IPI-adjacent diplomatic and policy background material — functioning as a repeated access channel. Epstein received introductions, briefings, and scheduling access while donating to IPI (the Hooper Fellowship; the disclosed $650K in undisclosed-at-the-time donations) and lending personally to Rød-Larsen ($130K, 2013). The records place Epstein in recurring proximity to senior diplomatic, financial, academic, and political figures across these years; examples include Bill Gates, Henry Kissinger, Larry Summers, Jes Staley, Leon Black, Ehud Barak, Thorbjørn Jagland, the Mongolian president, and Hamad bin Jassim. Background briefings exchanged via the same channels included German submarines, polio in Syria, Gates Foundation milestones, and *Foreign Affairs* articles on Trump. The corpus does not establish Rød-Larsen's intent.
2. **Family entanglement.** From at least mid-2012 onward, the records document a relationship extending well beyond institutional contact: Hilde stayed in Epstein's Paris apartment; Terje and Mona dined with Epstein several times a year; Eva Dubin briefed Epstein on Mona's professional name in March 2011 (it is Eva Dubin, not Mona, who appears in the corpus making that introduction); staff handled the family's passport and travel logistics; Epstein purchased Apple Watches and appears in records concerning art-related gifts and purchases involving Edward; a small package shipped to Stowe School appears in correspondence whose declared customs value was questioned by Epstein staff. Epstein edited Edward's college-application essays in October 2018 and a coordinated NYU advocacy effort followed, involving UAE Ambassador Lana Nusseibeh's January 2019 letter to NYU President Andrew Hamilton and NYU trustee Rita Hauser's communications with the Admissions Office. In April 2018 Rød-Larsen and Epstein corresponded about Rød-Larsen's medical recovery: *"I will for a couple of months have physiotherapy. I will call you later."*
3. **Will provisions.** The 8 August 2019 will, executed two days before Epstein's death at the Metropolitan Correctional Center, lists Edward and Emma Rød-Larsen as $5M devisees each. Earlier drafts in the corpus had Emma at $2M with Edward not listed; the final document doubled and added. (The Rød-Larsen–Epstein relationship was not yet publicly known at the time the will was signed; *Dagens Næringsliv*'s reporting and Rød-Larsen's IPI resignation came in October 2020.) The will is **probative of the relationship's seriousness, but not of motive**. The corpus does not establish whether the bequests reflected affection, obligation, leverage, estate planning strategy, or another rationale.

The corpus shows the relationship continuing into **2019** with no documented interruption. The January 2019 *"to-see in Paris"* list still names *"Terje"* months after Epstein's renewed federal indictment was being prepared.

## 8. Open Questions / What the Corpus Does NOT Show

- **Whether either Edward or Emma ever met any of Epstein's victims** or were ever present at any setting where trafficking activity occurred. The corpus contains no such evidence. This report is about the financial and diplomatic relationship.
- **Whether the $5M bequests were paid out** to Edward and Emma. The Epstein estate has been the subject of continuing USVI litigation; the corpus does not include the post-2019 estate accounts. (The will provisions are conditional on each child surviving Epstein.)
- **The identity of the November 2012 Norway visa applicant**, her relationship to Epstein, and whether she received the visa.
- **Whether Mona Juul personally communicated with Epstein.** The corpus shows her vCard repeatedly being passed to Epstein by Rød-Larsen, dinner invitations bearing both their names co-signed, and her professional introduction by Eva Dubin — but the EFTA corpus contains no email *from* Mona Juul to Epstein. The Norwegian government review and Økokrim charges indicate that Norwegian authorities considered the relationship serious enough to warrant institutional and criminal scrutiny beyond what the EFTA email corpus alone can resolve.
- **The disposition of the Bermuda 2011 trip.** Family passport details are catalogued; the corpus does not contain a clear record of the trip having been completed, who travelled, or where they stayed.
- **The "they had a brief stint at IPI"** reference in the Norwegian government memo ([EFTA00084224](https://epstein-data.com/EFTA00084224)). Identity, dates, and full context of the IPI photo-taking incident remain unclear from corpus alone.

## 10. Edward Rød-Larsen — In Memoriam

Edward Juul Rød-Larsen died at age 25. His death was registered on **29 April 2026** and reported publicly by Norwegian media on **30 April 2026**.

This report does not speculate about the circumstances of his death. It also does not treat Edward as a public actor in the same way as his parents. Most corpus records involving him concern events when he was a minor or young adult: school applications, internships, gifts, travel logistics, and Epstein's will. The public significance of those records lies in what they show about Epstein's access to, and influence around, a diplomatic family. They are not evidence that Edward committed wrongdoing.

The documentary record is of a young person who was *introduced* to Jeffrey Epstein — not the reverse.

---

## 11. Closing Frame

The corpus does not show **merely** a casual donor relationship. It shows an embedded relationship: institutional funding, diplomatic access, private travel logistics, family gifts, educational advocacy, medical updates, and estate provisions. The records do not establish criminal guilt or motive. They do establish that Epstein's relationship with Rød-Larsen crossed the boundary between professional access and family-level involvement.
