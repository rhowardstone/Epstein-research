# WILLIAM BARR: COMPLETE INVESTIGATION OF ALLEGATIONS IN THE EPSTEIN FBI FILES

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

William Pelham Barr appears in the Epstein FBI files in **three distinct capacities**: (1) as a subject of an NTOC (National Threat Operations Center) tip alleging he was present during abuses at Epstein's residence, documented in the FBI's own Prominent Names briefing; (2) as the sitting U.S. Attorney General who oversaw the federal response to Epstein's death in custody on August 10, 2019, including the death investigation under case number 90A-NY-3151227; and (3) as a figure with pre-existing connections to Epstein through his father Donald Barr, who hired Epstein to teach at the Dalton School, and through his former law firm Kirkland & Ellis, which represented Epstein in the 2008 Florida non-prosecution deal.

This investigation identified **55 documents** across the FBI file databases that reference William Barr directly or as Attorney General in connection with Epstein. The Barr allegations appear in **4 separate versions** of the FBI's Prominent Names briefing document, as well as in news clips, legal correspondence, situational reports, and internal FBI communications.

### 1.3 Analysis of the Allegation

The Barr allegation contains **two distinct components**:

**Component A -- The NTOC Tip (Abuse Allegation):** A named individual (identity redacted) filed a formal NTOC tip stating that both William Barr and Leon Black "were present during abuses." This is a direct allegation of presence during criminal conduct. The fact that the FBI briefing pairs Barr with Leon Black is significant because Black himself is the subject of separate, more detailed abuse allegations on the same page (Leon Black section), including allegations of forced massage and oral sex, and threats against victims.

**Component B -- The Model Event Encounter:** The same or a related individual stated she was at Epstein's residence for a "model event" and encountered Barr there. Barr allegedly told her he "wanted to see her next time he came." Additionally, at a separate point in time, Epstein asked this individual whether she had "ever met Barr" -- suggesting Epstein was aware of or facilitating contact between Barr and this individual.

### 1.4 Significance

- The allegation is **internally sourced** -- it appears in the FBI's own briefing materials prepared by agents for case review, not in media reports or public filings.
- The NTOC tip was filed by a **named individual** (not anonymous), which the FBI distinguishes from the "numerous anonymous NTOC's" noted at the bottom of the page.
- The "model event" detail is consistent with known Epstein patterns of using modeling events as recruitment opportunities, including through Jean-Luc Brunel's MC2 Model Management (see [EFTA01728258](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01728258.pdf)).
- The fact that Epstein asked the victim if she had "ever met Barr" suggests Epstein viewed Barr as someone he could connect victims with -- a pattern consistent with his known trafficking methodology.

### 1.5 Cross-Referencing the NTOC Tip Victim

The victim name in the Barr NTOC tip is redacted in all versions of the Prominent Names briefing. The redaction pattern shows "NTOC filed by [blank], stated Barr and" -- indicating a female name was removed. The same victim appears to have provided both Component A (the abuse allegation) and Component B (the model event encounter), as the text flows as a single narrative entry.

## SECTION 2: THE NTOC TIPS DOCUMENTS

### 2.1 [EFTA01660651](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01660651.pdf) and [EFTA01660679](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01660679.pdf) -- NTOC Tip Spreadsheets

These two documents are comprehensive NTOC tip logs that record all tips received by the FBI's National Threat Operations Center related to Epstein. They contain columns for: the tip allegation text, the named individuals, the FBI follow-up actions, and criminal history checks.

**Key finding:** The Barr-specific NTOC tip does not appear in the Trump-focused sections of these spreadsheets (pages 0-4 of both documents are dominated by Trump-related tips). The Barr tip appears to be logged elsewhere in the NTOC system, as the Prominent Names briefing references it but the available NTOC spreadsheet pages focus primarily on tips naming Trump.

### 2.2 Model Event Context from NTOC Tips

The NTOC tip spreadsheet ([EFTA01660651](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01660651.pdf), page 0) contains a related entry about modeling events at Epstein's:

> "At age 16, while modeling, caller attended 8 parties at Epstein's New York residence. On one occasion, caller reported she was sexually assaulted by Epstein. On another occasion, two twin brothers, Allen and Oren, lured caller and her friend upstairs but they escaped back downstairs."

This tip -- while about a different caller -- confirms the pattern of young models being invited to Epstein's New York residence under the pretense of "model events" or "parties," which is the same context described in the Barr allegation.

### 2.3 Victoria's Secret / Modeling Connection

The NTOC tips repeatedly reference Victoria's Secret models and modeling events as vectors for Epstein's trafficking. The Barr tip's reference to a "model event" connects to Epstein's known use of modeling industry connections through Les Wexner (Victoria's Secret), Jean-Luc Brunel (MC2 Model Management), and others.

## SECTION 3: BARR'S RECUSAL DECISIONS

### 3.1 The Recusal Question -- [EFTA00018398](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00018398.pdf)

A critical FBI file document ([EFTA00018398](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00018398.pdf)) contains a CNBC article from July 9, 2019, circulated internally within the DOJ/SDNY with "High" importance, detailing Barr's recusal decisions:

**Key facts from the document:**

> "Attorney General William Barr will not recuse himself from involvement in the new federal criminal prosecution of accused child sex trafficker Jeffrey Epstein, an official said Tuesday."
>
> "But Barr will recuse himself from an internal Justice Department probe of current Labor Secretary's Alex Acosta involvement in approving a controversial no-prosecution deal with the wealthy financier a decade ago."
>
> **Barr's own statement (quoted Monday):** "I'm recused from that matter because one of the law firms that represented Epstein long ago was a firm I subsequently joined for a period of time."
>
> "Barr joined that law firm, Kirkland & Ellis, in 2009. Kirkland & Ellis had represented Epstein in 2008, when he was being investigated in Florida."
>
> **DOJ clarification:** "Due to his prior association with Kirkland and Ellis, the Attorney General has and will remain recused from any retrospective review of the resolution of the earlier [Southern District of Florida] matter."

### 3.2 The Kirkland & Ellis Connection

Multiple documents detail the role of Kirkland & Ellis in the original Epstein non-prosecution agreement:

- **[EFTA00009016](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%207/EFTA00009016.pdf)** (Acosta deposition transcript): Ken Starr and Jay Lefkowitz of Kirkland & Ellis are identified as Epstein's defense counsel who met with prosecutors to argue against federal prosecution. Acosta testified about receiving calls from Kirkland & Ellis after the August 2007 charging letter.

- **[EFTA00013989](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00013989.pdf)** (Kirkland & Ellis letter): A June 19, 2008 letter from Kirkland & Ellis to prosecutors on behalf of Epstein, arguing against prosecution and invoking Brady requirements.

### 3.3 The Split Recusal -- Analysis

Barr's decision to recuse only from the retrospective review of the Florida deal, but NOT from the active prosecution or oversight of the Epstein case, meant that:

1. Barr maintained direct oversight of the SDNY's sex trafficking prosecution
2. Barr maintained oversight of the Bureau of Prisons where Epstein was held
3. Barr personally oversaw the death investigation after August 10, 2019
4. Barr was NOT recused from any matter where the NTOC tip alleging his presence at Epstein's during abuses would be relevant

### 3.4 Additional Recusal Context -- [EFTA00028149](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00028149.pdf)

Internal SDNY prosecution emails reveal that the Southern District of Florida had been "ordered recused by the DOJ from criminal matters concerning Mr. Epstein at a much earlier date" -- a fact that defense attorneys noted while pressing for information about the genesis of the SDNY investigation.

## SECTION 4: BARR AND THE DALTON SCHOOL / DONALD BARR

### 4.1 Grand Jury Subpoena to Dalton School -- [EFTA00009632](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%207/EFTA00009632.pdf)

Federal grand jury proceedings transcript reveals that prosecutors issued a subpoena to the Dalton School:

> "We have issued a Grand Jury subpoena to the Dalton school located in New York and at this time they do not have the records we have requested or could not locate those."

This subpoena was issued as part of the federal investigation into Epstein. The Dalton School is where **Donald Barr** -- William Barr's father -- served as headmaster from 1964 to 1974. Donald Barr hired Jeffrey Epstein to teach mathematics and physics at Dalton in approximately 1974, despite Epstein not having a college degree at the time. Epstein was approximately 20 years old when hired.

### 4.2 NYT Article Confirmation -- [EFTA00020787](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00020787.pdf)

The New York Times article from August 23, 2019 ("Barr Seized on Epstein Case as Doubts Mounted About Justice Dept."), which was circulated within SDNY, explicitly acknowledges both connections:

> "Even Mr. Barr had chance ties to Mr. Epstein: His old law firm, Kirkland & Ellis, had worked with Mr. Epstein on the Miami deal, and decades earlier, **Mr. Barr's father had hired Mr. Epstein to teach at Dalton, the elite private school in Manhattan.**"

### 4.3 No "Donald Barr" Direct References

A search of all three databases for "Donald Barr" returned zero results, indicating that the FBI files do not contain direct references to William Barr's father by name. The Dalton connection is referenced only through the grand jury subpoena and the NYT article.

## SECTION 5: BARR'S ROLE IN THE EPSTEIN DEATH INVESTIGATION

### 5.1 Case Number: 90A-NY-3151227

The FBI death investigation was opened under case number **90A-NY-3151227**, classified as "UNSUB(S); JEFFREY EPSTEIN - VICTIM; DEATH INVESTIGATION." This case file contains extensive documentation including:

- FD-302 interview reports of MCC correctional officers
- CART (Computer Analysis Response Team) evidence submissions
- Video system analysis from MCC
- Situational Reports (SITREPs)

### 5.2 Barr's Personal Involvement -- Timeline

**August 10, 2019 (Day of Death):**
- Epstein found unresponsive at approximately 6:30 AM EDT with a makeshift noose around his neck ([EFTA00147294](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00147294.pdf))
- Barr was at home when his chief of staff called ([EFTA00020787](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00020787.pdf))
- "Over multiple briefings an hour, Mr. Barr spent that Saturday angrily grilling his deputies"
- Barr directed FBI to investigate and OIG to look for protocol lapses
- Barr issued public statement: "Mr. Epstein's death raises serious questions that must be answered"
- Initial incident reports documented that **two MCC Correctional Officers falsified observation logs** for Epstein's cell for the overnight hours of 8/9-8/10/2019 ([EFTA00147294](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00147294.pdf))

**August 12, 2019 (FOP Speech in New Orleans -- [EFTA00023984](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00023984.pdf)):**
- Barr flew to New Orleans and delivered remarks at the Fraternal Order of Police's 64th National Biennial Conference
- Opening remarks: "I was appalled -- indeed, the entire Department was -- and frankly angry, to learn of the MCC's failure to adequately secure this prisoner. We are now learning of serious irregularities at this facility that are deeply concerning and that demand a thorough investigation."
- Stated: "Let me assure you that this case will continue on against anyone who was complicit with Epstein. Any co-conspirators should not rest easy."

**August 12, 2019 (SITREP -- [EFTA00147294](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00147294.pdf)):**
- VCTF, SDNY, and OIG concluded interview of MCC Warden -- "No significant information provided"
- NYO CART determined video of hallway leading to Epstein's cell block "does still exist"
- Four MCC staff agreed to interviews
- **"The two guards that were the first to discover Epstein have both referred the FBI/OIG to their attorneys and have not yet agreed to be interviewed"**
- FBI search of Little St. James Island initiated simultaneously
- A drone from an unknown point of origin monitored the search from close range

**August 19-20, 2019 (Investigation Updates -- [EFTA00019402](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00019402.pdf)):**
- Barr vowed "a full investigation into the federal prison"
- Barr stated he was "appalled... and frankly, angry to learn of MCC's failure to adequately secure this prisoner"

**August 20, 2019 (Internal FBI Email -- [EFTA00037228](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00037228.pdf)):**
- FBI interviewed inmate Steven Lopez at MCC about "possible shredding of Epstein documents" -- Lopez had no information
- FBI interviewed BOP guards, one of whom "stopped interview upon starting it asking for an attorney"
- OIG planned to serve "last outstanding subpoena to BOP Officer Kale"

**August 23, 2019 (NYT Profile -- [EFTA00020787](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00020787.pdf)):**
- Barr described as "personally overseeing the four federal inquiries" and "briefed on them multiple times a day"
- Barr suspended two prison employees, transferred the warden (Lamine N'Diaye), and appointed Kathleen Hawk Sawyer to run Bureau of Prisons
- Barr "defended the coroner's report, saying he had seen nothing to contradict it"
- **Unresolved:** "The Bureau of Prisons has not yet said whether security cameras in the cell block showed anyone entering and leaving Mr. Epstein's cell in the hours before he died"

### 5.3 Key Details from the Death Investigation Files

**The Cellmate Removal ([EFTA00147294](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00147294.pdf)):**
> "In the morning hours of 8/9/2019, Epstein's cell mate, Efrain Reyes, was released from the MCC, leaving Epstein alone in his cell."

- 7/10/2019: Removed from suicide watch after evaluation

## SECTION 8: CONFLICT OF INTEREST ANALYSIS

William Barr's involvement in the Epstein matter presents **multiple layers of conflict**:

| Conflict | Details |
|---|---|
| **Father hired Epstein** | Donald Barr, as headmaster of Dalton School, hired Epstein circa 1974 |
| **Law firm represented Epstein** | Barr joined Kirkland & Ellis in 2009; the firm represented Epstein in 2008 Florida case |
| **Subject of NTOC abuse allegation** | A named tipster alleged Barr was "present during abuses" at Epstein's residence |
| **Alleged encounter with victim** | Barr allegedly met a victim at a "model event" at Epstein's and expressed interest in seeing her again |
| **Oversaw death investigation** | Despite these conflicts, Barr personally oversaw the death investigation (90A-NY-3151227) |
| **No final report issued** | Neither the FBI nor OIG ever issued a final report on Epstein's death under Barr's tenure |

### 8.1 What Barr Recused From vs. What He Didn't

**Recused from:** Retrospective review of the 2008 Florida non-prosecution agreement (the Acosta deal)

**NOT recused from:**
- The active SDNY sex trafficking prosecution
- Oversight of the Bureau of Prisons (where Epstein was held)
- The death investigation
- Any investigation into the NTOC tip naming him personally

## SECTION 9: EVIDENCE GAPS AND UNANSWERED QUESTIONS

1. **The NTOC tipster's identity**: The name of the individual who filed the NTOC tip alleging Barr's presence during abuses is redacted in all versions. This individual was a named (not anonymous) complainant, distinguishable from the "numerous anonymous NTOC's" noted separately.

2. **Follow-up on the Barr NTOC tip**: The files do not contain any documented follow-up investigation of the Barr allegation. The Prominent Names briefing appears to be a summary document; the underlying investigative materials are not present in the released files.

3. **The "model event" details**: No additional documentation of the specific "model event" at Epstein's where Barr allegedly appeared has been identified in the files. The timing, location (New York vs. Palm Beach), and other attendees are not specified.

4. **Dalton School records**: The grand jury subpoena to Dalton School was met with a response that records "could not be located." Whether subsequent attempts were made to obtain these records is not documented.

5. **Death investigation final report**: As noted by Tucker Carlson in January 2023 ([EFTA00035791](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%208/EFTA00035791.pdf)), neither the FBI nor the OIG ever issued a public final report on Epstein's death, despite Barr's repeated promises of accountability and transparency.

6. **Video evidence**: The SITREP ([EFTA00147294](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00147294.pdf)) confirmed that some video of the hallway leading to Epstein's cell "does still exist on the system" -- but the final disposition and analysis of this video has never been publicly documented.

7. **The unmonitored phone call**: Epstein made a 20-minute call on an "unsecured and unmonitored line" to Karyna Shuliak on the evening before his death. The contents of this call have not been disclosed.
