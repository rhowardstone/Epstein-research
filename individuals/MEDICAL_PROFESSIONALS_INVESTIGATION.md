## Executive Summary

Jeffrey Epstein maintained a network of at least **48 identified physicians, dentists, therapists, and medical providers** who collectively appear in over **23,000 EFTA documents**. This network served three distinct functions:

1. **Personal medical care** — Two primary care physicians (Dr. Bruce Moskowitz, 1,251 docs; Dr. Bernard Kruger), a urologist (Dr. Harry Fisch, 588 docs), a cardiologist (Dr. Rony Shimony), an internist (Dr. Woodson Merrell), an infectious disease specialist (Dr. Cahill), a chiropractor (Dr. Perry Bard), and 15+ specialists on referral including spine surgeons (McCance, Hartl, Qureshi), allergists (Gupta, Busse, Lighvani), a physical therapist (Gage), and regional providers in Palm Beach, USVI, and New Hampshire
2. **Cosmetic and dental services for young women** — A dermatologist codenamed "Dr. Evil" (Dr. Steven Victor, 337 docs) who provided $20,000+ in free treatments to Epstein's "friends," plus Dr. David Orentreich (17 docs), Dr. Ellen Marmur (94 docs), Dr. Gervaise Gerstner, Dr. Chen (oral surgeon), Dr. Michael Reed (hair specialist — $725 paid by Epstein for young woman), Dr. Lee Ellen Morrone (appointments paid on "JE's Black Amex"), and a dental education pipeline through Dr. Thomas Magnani, Dean Ira Lamster, and Dean Christian Stohler at Columbia
3. **Institutional access and social capital** — Dr. Eva Andersson-Dubin (6,750 docs), Dr. Melanie Walker (221 docs), Dr. Boris Nikolic (13,969 docs), Dr. Mark Landon (6 docs), Dr. Henry Jarecki (psychiatrist sued by an Epstein accuser), Dr. Jess Ting and Robin Solomon at Mount Sinai, Dr. Mehmet Oz, and a science network including Deepak Chopra, Dean Ornish, Elkhonon Goldberg, Stuart Hameroff, and Ted Kaptchuk

The most significant finding is a **January 2018 text exchange** in which Dr. Moskowitz explicitly advises Epstein to send two women with gonorrhea to the JFK ER rather than treating them himself, stating: **"That way I do not have to report the cases to health department including contacts"** ([EFTA01613870](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01613870.pdf), p.3). This is the most direct evidence in the corpus of a physician deliberately structuring care to evade mandatory STI reporting requirements.

The EFTA corpus also reveals **zero medical/forensic subpoenas** were issued during the federal investigation — one of seven investigative tracks in the [Prosecutorial Query Graph](https://epstein-data.com/reports/pqg_lines_of_investigation/00_INDEX.html) that received no subpoena activity whatsoever.

### What This Report Does NOT Show

This report does not establish that any physician named herein had knowledge of Epstein's sex trafficking operation. Providing medical care to a patient — even a convicted sex offender — is not inherently wrongful. The report documents the evidence as it appears in the corpus, including contexts that raise questions about mandated reporting obligations, but does not make legal conclusions about any individual's liability.

## 1. The Mandated Reporting Failure

### 1.1 The Gonorrhea Thread (January 11-13, 2018)

The most important medical document in the corpus is an iMessage thread between Epstein (`jeeitunes@gmail.com`) and Dr. Bruce Moskowitz, extracted from Epstein's devices.

**Source**: [EFTA01613870](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01613870.pdf), pages 2-4

**January 11, 2018:**
> **Epstein**: "I wonder if this is either the chronic version and/or the drug resistant strain, and our culprit"
> **Moskowitz**: "DNA test will tell"
> **Moskowitz**: "Answer 2 gm azithromycin plus 1 gm rocephin and culture in two weeks"
> **Moskowitz**: "Will check with friend at cdc"

**Later that day:**
> **Epstein**: "I think to be safe my two friends should get shot by you tomorrow or send them somewhere close"
> **Moskowitz**: "Yes JFK ER North"
> **Moskowitz**: **"That way I do not have to report the cases to health department including contacts"**

**January 12, 2018:**
> **Epstein**: "Same"
> **Moskowitz**: "They say a friend overseas"
> **Epstein**: "Sorry, do they say their friend had gono"

**January 13, 2018:**
> **Epstein**: "Gonorhea rash?"
> **Moskowitz**: "Unlikely"

**Analysis**: Epstein refers to the infected women as "my two friends." He asks Moskowitz to administer antibiotic injections, but Moskowitz redirects them to the JFK ER, explicitly citing his desire to avoid health department reporting. In New York, gonorrhea is a **mandatory reportable disease** under the [NYC Health Code, Article 11, Section 11.03](https://www.nyc.gov/site/doh/providers/health-topics/stds.page) and [10 NYCRR 2.10](https://www.health.ny.gov/professionals/diseases/reporting/communicable/) — physicians must report cases to the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene, including information about contacts. By redirecting the women to an ER where he was not the treating physician, Moskowitz avoided triggering his own reporting obligation.

**What this does NOT show**: The text thread does not identify who the "two friends" are or their ages. It does not show that Moskowitz knew how the women became infected or whether they were trafficking victims. Gonorrhea transmission can occur through any sexual contact. The thread does show that Moskowitz's concern was specifically about the reporting obligation, not about the women's welfare.

**Corroborating context**: On March 26, 2019, a Business Insider article titled "super-gonorrhea is antibiotic resistant" was shared with Epstein in a separate iMessage thread ([EFTA01618036](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01618036.pdf), p.4). On May 19, 2019, Epstein shared a UW Medicine article on "antibiotic-resistant-gonorrhea" in a Skype conversation ([EFTA01619006](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01619006.pdf), p.3). This indicates sustained concern about STI exposure over at least 16 months.

### 1.2 Victor's Free Treatment Pipeline

The second mandated reporting red flag is Dr. Steven Victor's arrangement with Epstein. Victor treated multiple young women sent by Epstein at no charge, in exchange for Epstein's ongoing financial support of Victor's businesses.

**The deal** ([EFTA01779967](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01779967.pdf)):
> **Epstein to Victor** (June 10, 2010): "the last time I bailed you out we agreed I would never see another bill for my friends"
> **Victor to Epstein**: "So two more girls. According to my records I have done $20k on your group of friends. Need you to help now let me know"

Victor was a dermatologist seeing a rotating cast of young women referred by a convicted sex offender, performing cosmetic procedures for free as part of a financial arrangement. Under New York law, physicians are **mandated reporters** who must report suspected child abuse or maltreatment ([N.Y. Soc. Serv. Law § 413](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/SOS/413)). Whether Victor's patients included minors is not established in the corpus, but the pattern — a stream of young women sent by a man convicted of soliciting a minor for prostitution — raises serious questions about whether Victor had an obligation to inquire further.

**What this does NOT show**: The documents do not establish that any woman Victor treated was a minor or a trafficking victim. Victor's treatments appear to have been cosmetic (laser, acne medication, dermatology). The "friends" arrangement could reflect ordinary concierge medicine for wealthy clients. However, the volume (20+ women), the financial coercion (treatments contingent on Epstein's payments), and Epstein's criminal history create a pattern that should have triggered professional scrutiny.

### 1.3 Zero Medical Subpoenas

The [Prosecutorial Query Graph](https://epstein-data.com/reports/pqg_lines_of_investigation/00_INDEX.html) identifies seven investigative tracks used in the federal Epstein investigation. ## 7. Medical Control as a Pattern

### 7.1 The Investigative Gap

The prosecution issued **zero medical subpoenas** during the federal investigation — one of seven investigative tracks in the [Prosecutorial Query Graph](https://epstein-data.com/reports/pqg_lines_of_investigation/00_INDEX.html) that received no subpoena activity. No medical records were subpoenaed, no physician was called before the grand jury, no hospital records were sought. This is notable given the evidence of STI infections (Section 1.1), the volume of cosmetic treatments for young women (Section 3), and the OB-GYN charges on Epstein's AmEx (Section 6).

The EFTA corpus itself contains no victim medical records. This is expected — such records are protected by HIPAA and basic privacy considerations, and their absence from a public document release is appropriate, not suspicious. What *is* notable is that the prosecution apparently never sought them through sealed subpoenas either.

## Methodology

### Sources Searched
- **Full-text corpus** (2.73M pages, FTS5-indexed): Searched for all physician names, medical terms (doctor, physician, surgeon, gynecolog, psychiatrist, therapist, dentist, allergist, dermatologist, ophthalmologist, birth control, STD, gonorrhea, mandated reporter, prescription, pregnancy, nurse), and medical merchant names
- **[EFTA01671962](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01671962.pdf)**: Full 9,824-page AmEx statement parsed for medical merchant charges (Orentreich, Marmur, Gerstner, Krumholz, Positano, HSS, Mount Sinai, OB-GYN, labs, pharmacies)
- **Person registry** (1,538 persons with aliases — available at [Epstein-research-data](https://github.com/rhowardstone/Epstein-research-data)): Filtered for medical professionals (27 matches)
- **Existing reports**: All investigation reports grepped for medical/doctor references
- **Scheduling emails**: Systematic search of Groff-coordinated appointment emails for doctor names
- **iMessage threads**: Moskowitz (EFTA01613818-01613870), Kruger (EFTA01616758+), Shimony (EFTA01616676+), Fisch (EFTA01616196+) — multi-page chat exports with medical content
- **Web searches**: 10+ targeted searches for named physicians + mandated reporting coverage

### Source Reliability
- **Tier 1**: iMessage threads (EFTA01613870 gonorrhea), emails with headers, AmEx statements, Epstein's will — these are primary documents
- **Tier 2**: Daily Beast, Cornell Daily Sun, NBC News, Miami New Times, TMZ, Bloomberg reporting — professional journalism citing primary documents
- **Tier 3**: National Today, Primetimer (news aggregators repackaging Tier 2 reporting), Dr. Kara Fitzgerald (podcast/commentary), Jocalyn Clark/BMJ (academic analysis) — referenced for completeness but not relied upon for specific claims

### Privacy Note
This report names women who appear in the EFTA corpus as adults in Epstein's social and medical orbit (e.g., Karyna Shuliak, who is extensively documented as Epstein's girlfriend). Where women's identities are not already public, they are described generically ("unnamed woman," "a woman"). This report does not determine whether any named individual was a trafficking victim — the documents show medical relationships and financial control patterns, not legal status. The "two friends" in the Moskowitz gonorrhea thread (Section 1.1) are not identified.
