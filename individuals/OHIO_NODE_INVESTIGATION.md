## 2. The Deutsche Bank Document — An OCR Caution

The Ohio Node series [identifies a Deutsche Bank authorization form](https://thefalloutwithtbs.substack.com/p/the-ohio-node-the-intelligence-background) as listing Epstein as account holder with "an Atkins in beneficiary context."

[EFTA01366046](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01366046.pdf) is a genuine Deutsche Bank Private Wealth Management "Authorization for Asset Movement" form. The verified fields:

- **Account holder**: Jeffrey E. Epstein
- **Wire amount**: $100,000.00
- **Wire destination**: Citibank
- **Signed by**: Darren Indyke
- **Date**: October 23, 2015

However, the OCR-extracted text renders the form field "Recipient's Mailing **Address**" as "Recipient's Mailing **Atkins**." This is a consistent OCR artifact — the same misread appears on a second identical-format Deutsche Bank form at [EFTA01359377](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01359377.pdf), which is a $29,000 wire to Cecile R. de Jongh at First Bank, Puerto Rico, also signed by Indyke. Both forms use the same standardized Deutsche Bank Alex. Brown template, and both produce the same OCR garble on the pre-printed "Mailing Address" field.

**The word "Atkins" does not appear as a beneficiary, payee, or person on either form.** It is a pre-printed form field label that the OCR engine consistently misreads.

This does **not** undermine the broader Ohio Node thesis — Jim Atkins is documented in two FBI FD-302 interviews and one survivor journal as a real person connected to Epstein. But the specific claim of a Deutsche Bank document "naming an Atkins in beneficiary context" appears to originate from an OCR misread rather than an actual financial connection. **Readers should examine the [original PDF](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01366046.pdf) directly** to confirm whether the printed text reads "Address" or "Atkins."

### 7.1 Journal EFTA Citation

The Ohio Node series cites **EFTA02731370** for the "Mr. Atkins are not who they say" quote. This Bates number corresponds to page 9 of [EFTA02731361](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731361.pdf) (the first journal), which contains a trafficking clipping about Lake Placid — not the "Mr. Atkins" text. The correct citation is **EFTA02731426** (page 6 of document [EFTA02731420](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02731420.pdf)).

### 7.2 Deutsche Bank "Atkins" Wire

As detailed in [Section 2](#2-the-deutsche-bank-document--an-ocr-caution), the "Atkins" on the Deutsche Bank authorization form ([EFTA01366046](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01366046.pdf)) appears to be an OCR misread of the pre-printed form field "Recipient's Mailing Address." The same artifact appears on a second identical form ([EFTA01359377](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01359377.pdf)). Readers should examine the original PDF to resolve the ambiguity.

### 7.3 Weyerhaeuser Sale Price

The Ohio Node series reports: "she sold the position at $108.52 per share, recording a loss of $657.70." The JPMorgan statement at [EFTA01523186](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01523186.pdf) records the sale at **117.296** (% of par) with proceeds of $11,729.60 against a cost basis of $11,509.70 — a **realized gain of $219.90**. The source document does not support the reported sale price or loss figure.

### 7.4 Kerney and Caplinger Employer

The Ohio Node series describes Gary Kerney as "CEO of the New Albany Company" and Stephen Caplinger as "the New Albany Company's director of planning." The corpus identifies both as employees of **Landmark Land Company, Inc.** — Kerney as Senior VP ([EFTA01965817](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01965817.pdf)) and Caplinger as VP Design & Planning ([EFTA01780686](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01780686.pdf)). Landmark Land is a distinct entity from The New Albany Company LLC. Both companies had relationships with Epstein, but through different structures.

### 7.5 Cecil / Butcher Distinction

The FBI document describes the accountant Cecil as a Black male ([EFTA02858481](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2012/EFTA02858481.pdf), page 3). C.H. "Cecil" Butcher Jr. was a white Tennessee banker. These are apparently different people.

### 7.6 Bond Terminology

The Ohio Node series describes the Weyerhaeuser position as "10,000 shares." Corporate bonds are denominated in face value (par value), not shares. The position was $10,000 face value — one bond at par.
