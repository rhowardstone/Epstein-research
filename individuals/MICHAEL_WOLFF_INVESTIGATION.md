## Who He Is

Michael Wolff (b. 1953) is a journalist and author best known for *Fire and Fury: Inside the Trump White House* (2018) and *Siege: Trump Under Fire* (2019). He met Jeffrey Epstein in the late 1990s during the internet bubble and maintained a continuous, documented relationship from at least 2011 through Epstein's death in August 2019. Wolff claims to possess approximately 100 hours of recorded interviews with Epstein across 30+ sessions.

The EFTA corpus contains **72 documents with Wolff's email addresses** (highest confidence direct correspondence), plus **89 House Oversight emails** and hundreds of scheduling, calendar, and phone records — totaling over **4,000 documents** referencing Wolff across the corpus. The date range spans May 2011 through May 30, 2019 — six weeks before Epstein's final arrest. These documents reveal that Wolff operated not as a journalist covering Epstein, but as a strategic advisor, crisis manager, intelligence broker, social fixer, and counter-surveillance operative — while simultaneously writing profiles of his subject.

Wolff served as Epstein's de facto media strategist during at least three crisis periods, all occurring years after Epstein's 2008 sex offense conviction.

#### January 3, 2015: Virginia Roberts / Prince Andrew Allegations

When Virginia Roberts Giuffre's allegations naming Prince Andrew hit every British front page, Ian Osborne forwarded the coverage to Epstein. Wolff immediately engaged with detailed media strategy:

> "I would not act till then. And I would hit very hard on media's willingness to hide behind court documents and repeat all manner of preposterous allegations. > "I think that is a reasonably good approach. I might make the central point that court documents are a back door way to make protected libelous allegations. The same email thread includes a reference to scheduling Bill Gates: "Can you give me time middle of the month and schedule Gates? Obviously, no time like now to deal with this."

**Source**: [EFTA01791807](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2010/EFTA01791807.pdf)-01791810 (DS10)

Can you do it on Saturday morning in NYC? I will come with them. Can be done in 30 minutes." **Epstein kills it**: On March 31, 2015, Epstein pulled the plug: "sorry, but this will create many more problems than either you [or] i want to deal with. cannnot. must rethink, will call in 1 hr." ([EFTA02506207](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%2011/EFTA02506207.pdf))

## Summary Assessment

The documentary record reveals Michael Wolff as one of the most deeply embedded figures in Epstein's post-conviction network. Unlike other journalists who covered Epstein, Wolff simultaneously:

1. **Provided crisis communications strategy** during at least three major threat periods (2015, 2016, 2018)
2. **Monitored and reported on other journalists** investigating Epstein (Patterson, Connolly, BBC)
3. **Gave his subject editorial control** over a major magazine profile, then accepted its cancellation
4. **Served as social broker** between Epstein and political figures (Bannon, Ailes, Saudi Deputy Crown Prince)
5. **Conducted opposition research** on Epstein's targets (Rothschild, Trump financial disclosure)
6. **Referred young women** as "assistants" to a registered sex offender (two documented instances, November 2013)
7. **Participated in legal crisis calls** alongside Epstein's attorneys (Indyke, Ruemmler)
8. **Helped plan a rehabilitation documentary** featuring world leaders and Nobel laureates
9. **Shared #MeToo intelligence** predicting which figures would face allegations
10. **Received reciprocal access**: Epstein facilitated introductions to Chomsky, Watson, Richardson, Saudi royals, and White House intelligence

The question raised by Ellie Leonard — why Wolff won't release his 100 hours of Epstein recordings — should be understood in the context of this documentary record. The tapes would reveal not just what Epstein said, but the full nature of a relationship in which a journalist functioned as a consigliere.
