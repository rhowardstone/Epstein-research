# MAXWELL'S EXTERNAL PROCUREMENT PIPELINE
## Evidence Prosecutors Cited That Maxwell Arranged Women for Influential Men Outside the Epstein Enterprise

---

## OVERVIEW

The Epstein corpus contains emails that the FBI and SDNY prosecutors interpreted as Ghislaine Maxwell arranging access to women — and in the FBI's view in some instances young females — for other influential men in her social network. The emails were assembled from devices seized from Epstein's residences in 2019 and presented by prosecutors as trial evidence in *United States v. Maxwell* (20 Cr. 330). The court excluded them because the government could not tie them to the charged conduct involving minors and because the risk of confusing the jury outweighed any probative value. The prosecutors' theory about what the emails show, and the FBI's sworn interpretations of them, survive in the motion practice and hearing transcript.

**The core documentary record includes:**
- Four sequential emails between Maxwell and a person identified by SDNY as "Individual-5," in which Maxwell arranges female companions for Individual-5 and Individual-5 explicitly delegates the task to her
- A separate email from Maxwell to Doug Band (then a senior Clinton White House official) offering to arrange a dinner with two women Maxwell had selected
- A paid-recruiter email in which a third party solicits payment from Maxwell for identifying "beautiful, talented young women" to bring into the network
- An FBI search warrant affidavit interpreting all of the above as procurement of young females for sex acts
- A pretrial evidence notice (filed under Federal Rule of Evidence 404(b)) that, in one less-redacted public copy, explicitly names **the Duke of York** as the recipient of Maxwell's "2 legged sight seeing" arrangements

These exhibits were excluded from the Maxwell jury because the court found the government could not establish the women in those emails were minors and that any probative value was outweighed by the risk of confusing the jury. Authenticity was not the basis for exclusion reflected in the ruling. At least some of the emails survive in the corpus and in public derivative archives; the government's theory about them also survives in the motion practice and hearing transcript.

## THE INDIVIDUAL-5 COMMUNICATIONS (2002)

The FBI assembled four communications between Maxwell and "Individual-5" into a single affidavit paragraph. The affidavit is a search warrant application signed August 2, 2017 ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf)).

### Email 1: February 28, 2002 — "I leave that entirely to you"

Individual-5 emailed Maxwell about an upcoming trip Maxwell was helping to arrange. Individual-5 wrote:

> *"As for girls well I leave that entirely to you and [another individual]!"*

The FBI's sworn interpretation: "Individual-5 was discussing MAXWELL's attempts to arrange for young females to engage in sex acts with Individual-5." ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf), p. 34)

### Email 2: March 3, 2002 — "2 Legged Sight Seeing"

Maxwell emailed Individual-5 about an upcoming trip. The full text of this email was recovered from Epstein's devices and exists in the corpus at [EFTA00011441](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00011441.pdf). Maxwell wrote:

> *"I just gave Andrew your telephone no. He is interested in seeing the Nazca Lines. He can ride but it is not his favorite sport ie pass on the horses. Some sight seeing some 2 legged sight seeing (read intelligent pretty fun and from good families) and he will be very happy. I know I can rely on you to show him a wonderful time and that you will only introduce him to friends that you can trust and rely on to be friendly and discreet and fun. He does not want to read about any trip in the papers whom or what he saw. Call me if you have any questions - otherwise you can expect a very English sounding gentleman on the phone to call up and say hi. I told him it would be best if he made his plans directly with you. The only part that I am jealous about is that he will get to see you and that he will be in Peru and have you as his tour guide for a day."*

The recipient — "The Invisible Man" at `aace@dial.pipex.com` — replied the same day:

> *"Got it I will ring him today if I can. Love you A xxx"*

**The government's 404(b) letter, in a less-redacted copy preserved in the corpus ([EFTA00080134](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00080134.pdf)), explicitly names "the Duke of York" as the person for whom Maxwell made these "2 legged sight seeing" arrangements** — this is the primary identification, made by federal prosecutors. The filed exhibit copy in the court record does not preserve this wording; the identification comes specifically from EFTA00080134, a less-redacted version. The email address `aace@dial.pipex.com` is independently consistent with that identification: the initials A.A.C.E. correspond to Andrew Albert Christian Edward, the formal given names of Prince Andrew. The initials corroborate the government's identification but are not the basis for it.

The FBI's sworn interpretation: "MAXWELL was discussing her attempts to arrange for young females to engage in sex acts with Individual-5." ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf), p. 35)

**Copies of EFTA00011441 in the corpus:** [EFTA00068368](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00068368.pdf), [EFTA00087584](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00087584.pdf), [EFTA00099193](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00099193.pdf)

### Email 3: August 16, 2002 — "New Inappropriate Friends"

Individual-5 emailed Maxwell directly asking: *"Have you found me some new inappropriate friends?"*

The FBI's sworn interpretation: "Individual-5 was asking whether MAXWELL had found any young females to engage in sex acts with Individual-5." ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf), p. 34)

## THE DOUG BAND EMAIL (undated)

A single-page email from Maxwell to `doug.band@gsa.gov` — Doug Band's official U.S. General Services Administration email address, placing him in government service at the time — exists at [EFTA00011434](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00011434.pdf). Maxwell wrote:

> *"Philip just e mailed me your contact info. When we are all back in NY the plan is to do a dinner - we'll have the sluty Spanish girl which will be nicely counter balanced by the cool poised Swedish type. Let me know when you next go to Europe as I do have some interesting and fun friends that you could hook up with. Hopefully we will meet up again soon. Ghislaine"*

Doug Band served as a senior aide and "body man" to President Bill Clinton from 1995 through 2001 and remained on Clinton's staff through at least 2002 while co-founding Teneo Holdings in 2011. The `@gsa.gov` domain indicates this email was sent during his period of formal government employment. A civil case filing notes that Bill Clinton's phone numbers appear in Epstein's address book under "Doug Bands" [sic] ([EFTA00081180](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00081180.pdf)).

The government's 404(b) letter (see below) identified this as one of the seven emails demonstrating Maxwell "took steps to please other influential men by providing them with access to women she selected for them."

## THE PAID RECRUITER (February 2004)

A separate email chain from the same device search involves a third party designated "Individual-4." On or about February 7, 2004, Individual-4 emailed Maxwell asking about payment terms for identifying recruits. Individual-4 wrote:

> *"i have a couple of girls in mind, so let me know what arrangement we will have, and i will do my best to help"*

— in the context of soliciting payment for identifying *"beautiful, talented young women ... to train / work with"* Epstein.

The FBI's sworn interpretation: "Individual-4 was discussing with MAXWELL the recruitment of young females to engage in sex acts with EPSTEIN." ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf), p. 34)

This email is structurally distinct from the Individual-5 pipeline: Individual-4 was being *paid by Maxwell* to identify girls for Epstein, while the Individual-5 communications show Maxwell procuring for a third party outside the Epstein arrangement.

## GX-416: THE "LIST OF WOMEN" — WHAT IS KNOWN

Government Exhibit 416 is a document from NYCO24350 (Bates SDNY_GM_02736115) described in the October 28, 2021 pre-trial exhibit index as "List of Women." The government cited it in the 404(b) letter as evidence similar in kind to the excluded emails — evidence that Maxwell assembled profiles of women for powerful men.

**Content (from defense brief):** The Maxwell defense filing of November 12, 2021 ([DOJ-OGR-00006881](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00006881.pdf), p. 16) quoted the ages of the women profiled: *"adult women (aged, in the document, **35, 25, 23, and 25 years old**)"* — four women, all adults. The full text of their descriptions remains unextracted; the government's characterization ("descriptions of adult women") and the defense's age citation are the only content known from the record.

**Authorship dispute:** The defense argued the government offered no metadata exhibit comparable to GX-417-B, GX-418-B, or GX-420-B to establish Maxwell as author. The -B exhibits in the series represent Word document metadata including author fields. The absence of a GX-416-B metadata exhibit alongside GX-416 in the list (no GX-416-B appears anywhere in the pre-trial index — only "GX-416-B List of Women - Metadata" with production date 2021.10.28) may indicate the author field was blank, contested, or not Maxwell's name.

**Outcome:** GX-416 does not appear in the final November 23, 2021 trial exhibit index ([EFTA00085040](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00085040.pdf), [EFTA00021038](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00021038.pdf)). It was withdrawn. The government did not admit it at trial. The list of four women — who they were, their connection to Maxwell, and the purpose of the document — was never established in open court.

## THE COURT RECORD

### Step 1: Search Warrant Affidavit (August 2, 2017)

The FBI assembled the Individual-5 communications into paragraph 48 of a search warrant affidavit seeking expanded access to devices seized from Epstein's properties ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf)). This affidavit pre-dates Maxwell's indictment by three years and reflects the FBI's own analytical conclusions about the emails.

### Step 2: Maxwell Trial — 404(b) Letter (October 11, 2021)

SDNY prosecutors sent a letter to Maxwell's defense counsel identifying seven emails — GX-401 through 404, GX-409, GX-410, and GX-413 — as evidence they intended to introduce at trial. The letter stated:

> *"the Government may offer certain exhibits at trial that demonstrate that, in addition to the defendant's conduct with Jeffrey Epstein, the defendant took steps to please other influential men by providing them with access to women she selected for them."*

One copy of this letter — [EFTA00080134](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00080134.pdf) — preserves substantive content that was redacted from all other publicly filed versions ([EFTA00090463](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00090463.pdf), [DOJ-OGR-00005622](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00005622.pdf), [DOJ-OGR-00006584](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00006584.pdf)). The less-redacted version states:

> *"For example, in one email, the defendant made arrangements for **the Duke of York** to be introduced to 'pretty' girls for, as the defendant called it, '2 legged sight seeing.' In another email, the defendant told **Doug Band** that she was arranging a dinner for him and a [redacted] and a [redacted]."*

The letter was signed under United States Attorney Damian Williams by AUSAs Alison Moe, Lara Pomerantz, and Andrew Rohrbach.

**GX-416 — "List of Women" — was listed in the October 28, 2021 pre-trial exhibit index but is ABSENT from the November 23, 2021 final trial exhibit index.** It was withdrawn between those dates — the same window as the *in limine* hearing (the pretrial hearing on what evidence would be admitted at trial). The Maxwell defense brief filed November 12, 2021 ([DOJ-OGR-00006881](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00006881.pdf), p. 10) disputed government claims about this document: *"the government contends these emails are 'similar in kind' to a 'document' they claim that Ms. Maxwell drafted (GX 416) concerning descriptions of adult women. Ms. Maxwell strongly disputes that suggestion. Notably, as the Court can tell, the government did not offer any evidence (or any offer of proof in their Response) that this document was in fact"* [drafted by Maxwell — sentence continues on next page]. GX-416 was excluded along with GX-401-413.

### Step 3: *In Limine* Ruling — Excluded (November 15, 2021)

Judge Alison Nathan ruled on the motion to exclude at a pretrial hearing. The hearing transcript is at [DOJ-OGR-00007052](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00007052.pdf), pp. 55–57. The government's AUSA argued the emails showed Maxwell's "willingness to facilitate encounters between powerful men and women they would like" and that she "was eager to please wealthy and powerful men." The judge ruled:

> *"The government doesn't allege that the individuals in these e-mails have anything to do with the crimes charged nor could they on the assumption that they can't establish that any of the individuals are below the age of consent. As for 404(b), I am also not convinced. The government argues that the e-mails show motive, intent, plan, and knowledge. Any potential probative value here would be substantially outweighed by the possibility of confusing the jury. Thus, I grant the defendant's motion as to Government's Exhibits 401 through 404, 409, 410, and 413."*

The ruling turned entirely on the age of the women involved. The government could not prove the women in those specific emails were minors. The emails were therefore excluded as potentially confusing a jury assessing crimes against minors. The government's opposition brief ([DOJ-OGR-00006709](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00006709.pdf), pp. 36–38) acknowledged as much: "At trial, it may not be obvious to a jury that an adult woman would be willing to provide Jeffrey Epstein with access to young girls. These emails make clear that the defendant was willing to serve in such a role."

Authenticity of the emails was not the basis for the court's exclusion. The men named were never charged or publicly identified by prosecutors in the filed record. The government's own opposition brief notes the emails "involve two persons not interviewed by the Government" ([DOJ-OGR-00006709](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/OGR/DOJ-OGR-00006709.pdf), pp. 36–38) — the specific source for that claim.

## WHAT THE FBI AFFIDAVIT ESTABLISHES

The 2017 search warrant affidavit ([EFTA00076712](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00076712.pdf)) is significant because it is not a trial document — it is a sworn law enforcement statement made four years before Maxwell's trial, under oath, to a federal magistrate. Its interpretive claims carry a different evidentiary weight than advocacy briefs:

- Maxwell procured girls for Individual-5 across at least four documented communications spanning February–August 2002
- Individual-5 explicitly delegated procurement to Maxwell ("I leave that entirely to you")
- Individual-5 asked Maxwell directly whether she had found "new inappropriate friends"
- Maxwell described the procurement in her own words as "2 legged sight seeing (read intelligent pretty fun and from good families)"
- The FBI believed, based on training and investigation, that all of these communications described procurement of young females for sex acts

A less-redacted copy of the government's 404(b) letter preserved in the corpus ([EFTA00080134](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00080134.pdf)) named the Duke of York as the person for whom Maxwell made the "2 legged sight seeing" arrangements, making the identification of Individual-5 a documented government position, not speculation.

**Analytical notes on GX-422:**
1. **Author unknown** — written in the third person by an unidentified narrator ("I almost always see them together"). This is not Maxwell's own voice. The government used it to establish the relationship timeline (Tr.1494-96); Maxwell's defense presumably could not contest its authenticity since it came from a device in Epstein's possession.
2. **Dating the essay** — "the last 11 years" as a couple. The trial established the relationship began circa 1991. If the essay was written contemporaneously, it dates to approximately 2002. The NYCO24350 metadata (GX-422-B) would confirm creation date; the forensic notes confirm it was created under the "gmax" profile.
3. **"gmax" profile significance** — The essay was created by a user logged in as "gmax" on Epstein's Manhattan-residence hard drive (NYCO24350, Seagate IDE, FBI 1B32, July 2019 search). Maxwell authored or saved this third-party description of herself to a device in Epstein's New York townhouse.
4. **Purpose** — Most likely prepared for litigation (civil deposition, NPA negotiations), press/PR use, or as a relationship-status document. The tone is a character reference written by a social acquaintance.

**Note on numbering systems:** The SDNY_GM Bates numbers (e.g., SDNY_GM_02736489) and the DOJ corpus EFTA numbers are completely separate identifier systems that overlap in numerical range but refer to different documents. The EFTA numbers are assigned by the DOJ bankruptcy release process; the SDNY_GM numbers are the Maxwell trial Relativity database numbers. The actual emails (GX-401–413) cannot be located by treating their Bates numbers as EFTA numbers. The Doug Band email ([EFTA00011434](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00011434.pdf)) and the Prince Andrew email ([EFTA00011441](https://www.justice.gov/epstein/files/DataSet%209/EFTA00011441.pdf)) were found by content search, not by Bates-to-EFTA mapping.

## SEARCH SPINE — HOW THIS REPORT WAS ASSEMBLED

The following queries and cross-references produced the findings above. Documented for reproducibility.

**Query 1 — Entry point:**
```sql
-- Full-text corpus search for "influential" + "men" in Maxwell documents
SELECT efta_number, page_number, text_content FROM pages
WHERE text_content LIKE '%influential%men%' AND text_content LIKE '%email%'
```
→ Found: EFTA00016206 (Maxwell 404(b) motion), which disclosed existence of GX-401–413

**Query 2 — The 404(b) letter:**
```sql
-- Search for 404(b) letter copies
SELECT efta_number FROM pages
WHERE text_content LIKE '%404(b)%letter%' OR text_content LIKE '%Maxwell Rule 404%'
```
→ Found: DOJ-OGR-00005622, DOJ-OGR-00006584, EFTA00080134, EFTA00090463

EFTA00080134 identified as less-redacted copy through direct content comparison. Unique passage: *"...the Duke of York to be introduced to 'pretty' girls... Doug Band..."*

**Query 3 — In limine ruling:**
```sql
-- Find judge's exclusion ruling
SELECT efta_number, page_number FROM pages
WHERE text_content LIKE '%GX-401%' AND text_content LIKE '%grant%'
```
→ Found: DOJ-OGR-00007052 (November 15, 2021 hearing transcript), p.56–57

**Query 4 — Exhibit Bates numbers:**
```sql
-- GX-401 through 416 Bates mapping
SELECT page_number, text_content FROM pages WHERE efta_number='EFTA00024238'
AND text_content LIKE '%GX-40%'
```
→ EFTA00024238 (October 28, 2021 exhibit index) confirmed all Bates numbers

**Query 5 — Email content search:**
```sql
SELECT efta_number, page_number, text_content FROM pages
WHERE text_content LIKE '%Doug Band%' OR text_content LIKE '%2 legged%'
OR text_content LIKE '%sight seeing%'
```
→ Found: EFTA00011434 (Doug Band email), EFTA00011441 (Prince Andrew email), EFTA00076712 (FBI affidavit)

**Query 6 — Individual-5 corpus sweep:**
```sql
SELECT efta_number, page_number FROM pages
WHERE text_content LIKE '%Individual-5%' AND efta_number NOT LIKE 'DOJ-OGR%'
```
→ Confirmed 5 copies of FBI affidavit across EFTA00076712, 00091454, 00153059, 01625916, 01653490

**Query 7 — Source system identification:**
```sql
-- NYCO24350 as source for all GX-401 series
SELECT page_number, text_content FROM pages WHERE efta_number='EFTA00088936'
AND text_content LIKE '%NYCO%'
```
→ Confirmed NYCO24350 as forensic image source for all GX-401–422 exhibits

**Query 8 — Device hardware and user profile:**
